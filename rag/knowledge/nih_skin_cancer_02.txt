TOPIC: Melanoma Warning Signs and ABCDE Rule
---
Melanoma is the deadliest form of skin cancer. Dermatologists use the ABCDE rule to help identify suspicious moles or lesions that may be melanoma:

A — Asymmetry: One half of the mole does not match the other half.
B — Border: The edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred.
C — Color: The color is not uniform and may include shades of brown, black, pink, red, white, or blue.
D — Diameter: The spot is larger than 6 millimeters (about the size of a pencil eraser), although melanomas can be smaller when first detected.
E — Evolving: The mole is changing in size, shape, or color, or any new symptom such as bleeding, itching, or crusting develops.

Any mole or skin lesion that fits one or more of these criteria should be evaluated by a doctor immediately. Early melanoma found before it spreads is nearly 100% curable with surgical removal.

Other warning signs include: a sore that does not heal within 3 weeks, a spot or sore that continues to itch, hurt, crust, or bleed, or areas of skin that look different from surrounding skin.

In Bangladesh, patients with these warning signs should seek care at a district hospital or medical college hospital dermatology department without delay. Do not rely on this app for skin cancer screening — this app screens for common benign skin conditions only.
